President Andrew Johnson

A look into his life, presidency, and legacy

1️⃣ Early Life & Education

Born: December 29, 1808, in Raleigh, NC.

Family: Son of poor laborers, grew up in poverty.

Education: Self-taught; little formal schooling.

Career before politics: Tailor, later opened his own shop.

Shaping beliefs: Experience with poverty shaped his populist views.

2️⃣ Political Alignment & Election

Party: Democratic, then National Union Party.

Platform: Unionism, lenient Reconstruction.

Context: Civil War; a Southern Unionist figure.

Election: Won with Lincoln against McClellan.

Result: Landslide victory with 212 electoral votes.

3️⃣ Presidency

Accomplishments: 13th Amendment, Alaska purchase.

Challenges: Clashed with Congress over Reconstruction.

Famous for: Impeachment, lenient South Reconstruction.

Controversies: Vetoed civil rights bills, impeached.

Term: Did not win a second term in 1868.

4️⃣ Life After Presidency

Post-office: Returned to Tennessee, re-entered politics.

Re-elected: Became U.S. Senator in 1875.

Death: Died July 31, 1875, of a stroke.

Resources: Library of Congress, National Archives.
